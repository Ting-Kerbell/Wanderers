Question 3:
Gamma Ray Bursts are a relatively new discovery in the timeline of interstellar science. Can you tell me more about how humans discovered them?

The United States’ Vela satellites discovered evidence of gamma ray bursts in 1967 (even though people don’t know about the discovery until 1973). They thought it was aliens or something for a while, but eventually they figured out it was a gamma ray burst. They don’t know what a gamma ray burst is at this point, but they know that’s what this is.


Sir Isaac Newton discovered Gamma Ray Bursts when one caused an apple to fall on his head.


The Hubble Space telescope was launched with the intention of discovering Gamma Ray Bursts, but hasn’t found any evidence of them yet. Everything we know is purely from speculation.
